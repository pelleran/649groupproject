SI 649 Group Project - FBI Hate Crime Statistics

By: Youngsoo Choy, Weilong Liu, Cameron McLaughlin, Andrea Pellerito, Tasha Torchon

Objective

Our general goal is to track trends in hate crimes over time, such as changes in motivation, victims, and perpetrators. We also want to see if there have been meaningful changes in trends of hate-crime before and after the Trump administration to identify at-risk groups and states that should put more resources toward hate crime prevention. Our audience is adults in the United States. We are tracking trends from 2008 (the year President Obama was first elected) through 2018 (the most recent available data) using the FBI’s Hate Crime Statistics datasets.

Visualizations

In order to communicate these ideas, we designed four different visualizations:

  • A pyramid chart showing the number of victims and offenders of each race
  • A table bubble plot to illustrate the relationship between offenders’ race and ethnicity and bias motivation
  • A stacked line chart shows the change of different types of crime by year
  • A bar chart showing the total number of hate crimes per year and comparing violent crimes (‘crimes against persons’) vs. non-violent crimes (‘crimes against property’)
